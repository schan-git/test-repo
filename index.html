Goals

This document is meant as a specification for unifying visual and functional aspects of GUIs across all of Geist software. Emphasis is on detailing the visual presentation of common UI components, establishing consistency in data presentation, and defining navigational patterns. The goal is to achieve a unified look and feel throughout Geist software that is aesthetically pleasing and easy to use.
Priorities, in general order of weight, are:
  • Feasibility of development. Obviously, if a design cannot be built, it won't be. Designs requiring disproportionately long development time relative to their benefit to the product are not acceptable.
  • Consistency. This important both in UX as well as in engineering. Main points of consideration include:
    • - Internal consistency across Geist software.
    • - Consistency with use models and metaphors commonly found on the web.
    • - Consistency within the code and maintainability of code base.
    • - Current and projected consistency of browser support.
  • Usability. (Stuff not covered by consistency.)
  • Aesthetics/UX.Longevity, marketing compliance (brand recognition). Aesthetic life cycle of a software product moves more slowly than a marketing campaign. Not the specifics of Marketing style guide, but giving the impression of likeness to corporate image.
While taken into consideration where relevant, generalized usability principles, flavors of user experience, and anticipated workflow and specific use cases are not of immediate focus in this document. Such issues may be more fully addressed in a separate document and/or future phase.

Data Formats

Timestamps, dates, and times of day display in a standard format of YYYY-MM-DD hh:mm:ss XM ZZZ
- YYYY = four-digit year
- MM = two-digit month
- DD = two-digit day of month
- hh = two-digit hour (1-12)
- mm = two-digit minutes
- ss = two-digit seconds
- XM = AM/PM
- ZZZ = time zone
- In contexts where seconds are insignificant (e.g., defining time ranges, where seconds typically default to 00), seconds can be left out. When a time of day is displayed in the format aa:bb XM ZZZ, it should be assumed that aa=hh and bb=mm.
- User entry format should match display format. (insert form example)
Periods of time display in a standard format of (#) days (#) hr (#) min (#) sec
- This frequently requires a conversion from whatever timeticks format raw data may be in.
- Display begins with the first non-zero entity. E.g., display "1 min 30 sec"; not "0 days 0 hr 1 min 30 sec". Also, display "1 hr 0 min 5 sec"; not "1 hr 5 sec".
- User entry format should match display format. (insert form example)
Formats are open to discussion ... Any potential problems with formats currently suggested?
- Are milliseconds ever displayed?
- Do customers ever want 24-hour clock instead of AM/PM?
